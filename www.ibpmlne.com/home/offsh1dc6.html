BPM Regular Saver Account

If you would like to save between $10 and $300 per month, and earn an incredible 5.00% A.E.R.* variable on your savings, then the BPM Regular Saver Account is worth checking out. As well as this great rate of interest, you can access your savings at any time - without penalty.

How the BPM Regular Saver works?..

  • With the BPM Regular Saver Account, you can choose to save any amount from $10 to $300 in one lodgement every month.
  • You also have the flexibility to withdraw your money from this account at any time you like. There is no limit to the amount you can withdraw or no penalties for doing so. Your savings can be easily accessed through your BPM Branch or BPM Phone & Internet Banking.
  • To open an BPM Regular Saver Account, you must have a suitable BPM payment account, such as a Demand Deposit Account.
  • You will receive a variable rate of interest, which is calculated daily and paid into your account twice annually in April and October. For all Regular Saver Accounts opened since 19th February 2008, the AER will at least match ECB + 2.5% until 30th September 2009, and thereafter at least match ECB until 1st January 2010.
  • This offer applies to new Regular Saver Accounts opened after19th February 2008.
  • You will receive a statement on this account after the first transaction, and annually thereafter.
  • No fees or charges apply to the BPM Regular Saver Account.

For Regular Saver Accounts opened after 19/02/2008:

  • A customer may open one sole account or two joint accounts.
  • If, before 19th February 2008, a customer already has a Regular Saver Account, the customer may open one more Account, and joint customers may open two more joint Accounts. Condition 1.3 of the Terms and Conditions for Regular Saver Accounts is modified accordingly.

Customer Benefits:

  • The BPM Regular Saver Account is a straightforward and flexible account for regular monthly savings - with no hidden restrictions.
  • You can avail of the super variable interest rate of 5.00% A.E.R.
  • You will receive a variable rate of interest, which is calculated daily and paid into your account twice annually in April and October.
  • You have instant access to your savings - you can withdraw any amount (no minimum, no maximum) from your account via BPM Phone & Internet Banking or your BPM branch.
  • You may take a payment holiday (i.e. cease your payments into your Regular Saver account) at any time, without penalty.
  • You can change your payment amount at any time, without penalty.

How to open an BPM Regular Saver Account:

  1. Call our dedicated savings team on +447045757362 from Monday to Friday 8.00 - 21.00 hours and Saturday 9.00 - 18.00 hours (see 'Please note' below).
  2. Print an application form from our 'Next Steps' section above, fill in your details and post it to your branch (see 'Please note' below). Before you print the application form please ensure you read BPM Bank's Terms of Business.
  3. Call into your local BPM Branch to open an BPM Regular Saver Account.

Please note: You must have an existing BPM account to nominate as your payment account. If you are not an existing BPM customer, and do not have an existing BPM Current, Cashsave or a Demand Deposit account, you will need to visit your local BPM branch. You will need to bring two pieces of identification with you (a passport or full drivers licence for photographic identification and a recent utility bill or bank statement to prove your address) so that we can open a payment account as well as an BPM Regular Saver Account for you.

Tax on Interest earned on Regular Saver Accounts

We will deduct Deposit Interest Retention Tax (DIRT) at the prevailing rate from all interest earned, unless you are entitled to exemption from DIRT, and we will pay this directly to the Revenue Commissioners.

To the extent that DIRT has been deducted there is no further liability to Irish income tax, but you may be subject to PRSI/Health levy.

The Bank will deduct DIRT from the accounts of non-residents unless a fully completed non-resident declaration form is held by the Bank and a minimum balance of $12,500.00 is maintained at all times in each account maintained by the non-resident.

If you or your spouse are aged 65 or over in the current tax year, or are permanently incapacitated, and you are exempt from income tax, you may apply to have the interest on the account paid without deduction of DIRT.
